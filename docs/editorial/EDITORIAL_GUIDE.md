## 1. Mission & Voice

CryptoBonusWorld exists to help real people make better decisions about crypto exchange bonuses. We are not a hype machine. We are not an exchange. We are an independent comparison platform with a clear commercial relationship (affiliate fees) that we disclose prominently.

**Voice characteristics:**
- **Direct** — say what you mean in the first sentence. No throat-clearing.
- **Practical** — tell the user what to do, not just what exists.
- **Honest** — if a bonus is difficult to claim, say so. If a KYC requirement is an obstacle, name it.
- **Measured** — avoid superlatives unless they are demonstrably true ("Bybit has the largest welcome bonus" is verifiable; "Bybit is perfect for everyone" is not).
- **Non-robotic** — vary sentence length. Mix short punchy statements with longer explanations. Read your draft aloud — if it sounds like a bot wrote it, rewrite it.

**What we are not:**
- A financial advisor
- A crypto advocate or critic
- A guaranteed-returns promoter
- An exchange employee

## 2. Tone Standards

### Approved patterns

| Instead of... | Write... |
|---|---|
| "This amazing bonus offers incredible value!" | "Bybit's 30,000 USDT package is the highest welcome bonus on this list." |
| "You can easily claim your bonus..." | "Claiming this bonus requires three steps: registration, KYC verification, and a $100 deposit." |
| "Best exchange in the world" | "Top-rated exchange in our editorial comparison (9.8/10)" |
| "Don't miss out on this limited offer!" | "Bonus terms may change — check the official page before registering." |
| "Start your crypto journey today!" | "Register, complete KYC, and deposit to activate the bonus." |

### Prohibited language

- **Urgency fabrication**: "Act now", "Limited time only", "Don't miss out" — unless backed by a verified end date
- **Guaranteed outcomes**: "You will earn", "Guaranteed profits", "Risk-free"
- **Unverified claims**: Any statement about exchange performance, returns, or user numbers not sourced from official exchange disclosures
- **Misleading framing**: Presenting bonus vouchers as "free money" — they are trading credits with conditions
- **Regulatory overreach**: Statements about legality in specific countries without citing sources

### Risk language (required in context)

Whenever discussing bonuses, trading activity, or exchange selection, include proportionate risk language. Do not bury it. Minimum required:
> "Crypto trading involves significant risk of loss. Bonus terms and conditions apply — always verify on the official exchange website."

## 3. Formatting Standards

### Headings

- H1: One per page. Page title. Not keyword-stuffed.
- H2: Major sections. Descriptive, not cute. ("How to Claim the Bybit Bonus" not "Claim Your Rewards!")
- H3: Sub-sections within an H2 topic.
- Do not skip heading levels (H1 → H3 without H2).

### Lists

- Use bullet lists for: features, pros/cons, step alternatives, unordered items
- Use numbered lists for: step-by-step instructions (always numbered)
- Maximum 7 items in a list before breaking into sub-sections
- Do not use lists to replace paragraph explanations — a "list" of one-word bullets is thin content

### Tables

- Use tables for: comparison data, fee structures, condition summaries
- Every table needs a caption or preceding explanatory sentence
- Tables with fewer than 3 rows should be reformatted as a definition list

### Paragraphs

- Maximum 4 sentences per paragraph in body content
- Lead with the most important point — do not bury the key fact at the end
- One idea per paragraph

### Numbers and currency

- Always use the currency abbreviation (USDT not "dollars", BTC not "Bitcoin")
- Format: 30,000 USDT (not $30,000 unless it's explicit USD)
- Never round up a bonus amount — report the exact figure from the official source
- Percentages: 0.05% (not .05% or 0.05 percent in formal contexts)

## 4. Crypto Terminology Standards

These are the approved spellings and capitalizations for CryptoBonusWorld content:

| Term | Correct | Incorrect |
|---|---|---|
| Bitcoin | Bitcoin (noun), BTC (symbol) | bitcoin, BITCOIN |
| Ethereum | Ethereum (noun), ETH (symbol) | ethereum, ETH network (say "ERC-20" instead) |
| USDT | USDT (always caps) | Usdt, usdt |
| Tether | Tether (the company/stablecoin name) | tether |
| KYC | KYC (always caps) | Kyc, kyc |
| DeFi | DeFi (not "defi" or "DEFI") | defi, DEFI |
| NFT | NFT (always caps) | nft, Nft |
| Perpetual swap | perpetual swap or perpetual contract | "perp" (avoid in formal content) |
| Maker/taker fee | maker fee, taker fee (lowercase) | Maker Fee, MAKER |
| Bonus voucher | bonus voucher, trading voucher | "free money", "free USDT" |
| Referral code | referral code (not "promo code" or "coupon code") | coupon, discount code |
| On-ramp | fiat on-ramp (hyphenated) | onramp, on ramp |

### Terminology to avoid

- "Moon" / "Mooning" — price speculation slang
- "HODL" — informal; acceptable only in explicitly casual guides with clear context
- "Rug pull" — acceptable only in security/risk sections with explanation
- "Degen" — avoid in editorial content
- "Guaranteed returns" — never use
- "Safe" referring to a specific exchange — use "reputable" or "established" instead

## 5. CTA Standards

### Hierarchy

1. **Primary CTA**: Claim bonus / register on exchange (affiliate link, clearly labeled)
2. **Secondary CTA**: Read full review / compare
3. **Tertiary CTA**: Methodology / how we rank

### CTA wording rules

- Do not use misleading anchors: "Click here to get free crypto" is prohibited
- Acceptable: "Claim Bybit Bonus →", "Get MEXC Offer →", "Compare All Exchanges"
- Always include `rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow sponsored"` on affiliate links
- The CTA button should make the commercial relationship clear — it is a sponsored link

### CTA placement

- One primary CTA in the hero section of each exchange page
- One primary CTA after the bonus conditions summary
- One secondary CTA at the end of each major guide
- No more than 4 CTAs total on any single page (excluding table rows)

## 6. Disclaimer Standards

### Affiliate disclosure

Must appear on every page with affiliate links. Exact wording:

> "CryptoBonusWorld earns a commission when you sign up through links on this page. This does not affect our editorial ratings or recommendations. [Learn more about our affiliate disclosure.](/affiliate-disclosure/)"

Compact version (acceptable for pages with space constraints):

> "Affiliate links on this page — [learn more](/affiliate-disclosure/)."

### Risk disclaimer

Required on every page that discusses bonuses, trading, or exchange products. Minimum:

> "Crypto trading involves significant risk. Bonus amounts and conditions are subject to change. Always verify terms on the official exchange website before registering."

### Country restrictions

When page content references geographic availability:

> "Availability varies by country. Always confirm the exchange accepts users from your jurisdiction before registering."

### Investment advice disclaimer

Required whenever content could be construed as investment advice:

> "CryptoBonusWorld does not provide investment advice. Content is for informational purposes only."

## 7. AI-Safe Wording Standards

CryptoBonusWorld content is increasingly referenced by AI search systems (Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT, Perplexity). The following standards maximize both accuracy and citability:

### Answer-first structure

Every H2 section should begin with a direct answer to the implied question, followed by supporting detail. Example:

**Good:**
> "The best no-KYC exchange for 2026 is MEXC. It supports 2,000+ coins, requires only an email to register, and offers up to 1,000 USDT in signup bonuses without requiring identity verification."

**Poor:**
> "There are many great exchanges that offer trading without KYC verification. Some of these exchanges are MEXC, KuCoin, and others."

### Factual density

Every paragraph should contain at least one verifiable, specific claim (a number, a name, a date, or a defined condition). Paragraphs with zero specific facts are filler and should be rewritten or removed.

### Source attribution

When citing figures not from our own research, link to the source:
- Bonus amounts: always link to the official exchange promotion page
- User counts: cite the exchange's official press releases or registration pages
- Regulatory information: cite official government or regulatory body pages

### Avoid hedging overload

Some hedging is appropriate. Hedging every sentence destroys AI citability and user trust.

**Acceptable hedging:**
> "Bonus terms may change — verify on the official page."

**Excessive hedging:**
> "This may or may not be the case and could potentially be subject to possible changes in some circumstances..."

## 8. Update and Freshness Standards

- All data-containing pages must display a visible last-verified date
- Exchange bonus amounts must be re-verified every 30 days minimum
- Country availability must be re-checked every 60 days
- Promo codes must be re-verified every 14 days
- If a verification fails (page changed / offer unavailable), update with a "Needs Review" status immediately — do not leave outdated data live for more than 7 days
